01 Executive Summary

The Opportunity

Dev Weekends, a volunteer-driven tech community operating since 2020, proposes a strategic partnership with your university to establish a student-led tech community on campus, powered by our Ambassador Program.

This initiative leverages our existing infrastructure, mentor network, and proven curriculum to provide world-class technical training and career support at zero cost to the university and zero cost to students.

Partnership Fundamentals
Model: Ambassador-led campus team with central support
Cost to University: Completely free
Financial Requirements: None required
Delivery: Hybrid — on-campus events with remote mentorship
Focus Areas: Job Placements, International Programs, Coding Competitions, Hackathons
Projected Outcome: 92% placement success rate across partner campuses
The Imperative

Students across universities possess exceptional talent and determination. However, access to quality technical mentorship, industry connections, and career guidance remains limited. The gap between academic curriculum and industry requirements continues to widen, leaving graduates unprepared for the competitive job market.

Our programs offer a pathway to international opportunities, remote jobs, and professional growth that transcends traditional constraints. We believe that talent is everywhere — opportunity is not. This partnership aims to bridge that gap.

Through this collaboration, your university's students will gain direct access to mentors from Microsoft, Upwork, and leading global startups — along with structured preparation for programs like Google Summer of Code (GSoC), ICPC, hackathons, and international job placements.

02 About Dev Weekends

Who We Are

Dev Weekends is a community that builds engineers who change lives. We don't just teach code — we provide comprehensive mentorship, world-class training, and a support system that has helped hundreds of students break into the international tech industry.

Founded in 2020, we started with a simple belief: talent is everywhere, opportunity is not. What began as weekend coding sessions for a small group of students has grown into a national movement. Today, we operate across 60+ university campuses with a community of 20,000+ members and 800+ successfully placed engineers.

Our community is built on the principle of "give back" — successful engineers who were once mentees now return as mentors, creating a sustainable cycle of knowledge transfer and opportunity. Every program, workshop, and mentorship session is delivered completely free because we believe education should never be a barrier to success.

03 The Partnership Model

Ambassador-Led Campus Team

The heart of this partnership is our Ambassador Program. Your university will have a dedicated campus team — selected collaboratively by university stakeholders and Dev Weekends — that operates as the bridge between students and our global community.

This campus team reports directly to our Ambassador Program Head, who facilitates all support including curriculum resources, career planning workshops, technical training sessions, mock interviews, and guidance for programs like GSoC, ICPC, hackathons, and job placements.

Campus Team Structure
Lead Ambassador: Primary liaison with Dev Weekends HQ, oversees all campus activities
Technical Lead: Coordinates workshops, study groups, and technical sessions
Events Coordinator: Organizes hackathons, competitions, and mentor sessions
Community Manager: Drives engagement, social media, and member onboarding
What the Campus Team Delivers
  • Weekly Tech Circles: Regular meetups for coding practice, project sharing, peer learning, and collaborative problem-solving.
  • Monthly Workshops: Hands-on sessions on React, Node.js, AWS, Docker, Kubernetes, and AI/ML with practical exercises.
  • Hackathons & Competitions: Quarterly on-campus hackathons with recognition, certificates, and hiring partner connections.
  • Mentor AMA Sessions: Monthly Ask-Me-Anything sessions with industry professionals on careers, technical challenges, and insights.
  • Career Workshops: Resume reviews, LinkedIn optimization, mock interviews, and job search strategies.
  • Competition Prep: Dedicated tracks for ICPC, GSoC proposal writing, Outreachy, LFX Mentorship, and MLH Fellowship.
  • Study Groups: Organized DSA practice groups, system design discussions, and peer accountability sessions.
Support from Dev Weekends HQ
  • Curriculum & Resources: Training materials, workshop templates, presentation decks, and continuously updated learning paths.
  • Mentor Access: Direct connections to 45+ industry mentors for 1:1 sessions, code reviews, and career guidance.
  • Career Services: Mock interviews, personal referrals to hiring partners, job board access, and placement support.
  • Program Guidance: Step-by-step playbooks for GSoC, ICPC, hackathons, and international programs.
  • Monthly Strategy Calls: Regular check-ins with the Ambassador Program Head to review progress and plan events.
  • Marketing Support: Social media templates, event promotion materials, and brand assets.
  • Recognition & Rewards: Certificates, swag, spotlight features, and recommendation letters for top performers.

05 University Benefits

Value for Your University

This partnership creates measurable value for your institution — enhancing student outcomes, industry reputation, and placement statistics without any resource investment.

Enhanced Placement Rates

92% placement success across partner campuses. Students receive direct referrals and interview preparation from industry professionals. Many secure positions before graduation with salary packages 2-3x higher than typical local rates through remote international opportunities.

Competition Excellence

Improved performance in ICPC, ACM contests, and hackathons through structured competitive programming training. Partner universities have seen students qualify for ICPC regionals and win hackathons — reflecting positively on institutional rankings.

International Exposure

Students gain visibility through GSoC, Outreachy, and global open source programs. Each international fellowship brings recognition to both the student and the university, attracting prospective students and industry attention.

Industry Connections

Direct access to mentors from Microsoft, Upwork, and leading startups strengthens university-industry relationships. These connections can lead to guest lectures, industry visits, and preferential hiring pipelines.

Student Engagement

Active tech communities increase student engagement and satisfaction. Participants report higher confidence, better peer networks, and a stronger sense of belonging — contributing to improved retention.

University Support Required

To ensure the success of this partnership, we request the following support:

  • Official Recognition: Formal acknowledgment as a university-affiliated student organization or partner program
  • Facility Access: Access to classrooms, labs, or auditoriums for workshops and events (as available)
  • Faculty Advisor: A faculty member (preferably from CS/IT department) to serve as institutional liaison
  • Communication Support: Promotion through university channels — newsletters, notice boards, student portals
  • Team Selection: Collaboration in identifying and approving the campus ambassador team

Note: No financial contribution is required. All activities, resources, and support are provided completely free by Dev Weekends.
